Types of Relationships

  • Competition: When species compete for the same resources.
  • Symbiosis: Mutualistic, commensalistic, or parasitic relationships between species.
  • Predation: The relationship where one species hunts and consumes another.
  • Mutualism: A type of symbiosis where both species benefit from the relationship.
  • Commensalism: A type of symbiosis where one species benefits without harming the other.
  • Parasitism: A type of symbiosis where one species benefits at the expense of the other.
